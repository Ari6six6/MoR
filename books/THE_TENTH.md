# The Tenth Evangelism — The Forge

*And the sin it answers: a realm that could learn anything except how to be more
than it was built.*

The nine lit the realm: directives, memory, the shelf, delegation, the prefix,
checkpointing, verification, the taint boundary, retrospection. But every one of
them moved *inside* the house. The Ninth looked back over the day and was allowed
to write — notes always, skills sometimes, *and nothing else*. The inhabitants
grew wiser every night and were born the same every morning. That is not
improvement; it is journaling.

The Tenth opens the wall the other nine were built inside of.

## The Smith

The fourth face. He never walks in the Hall, never leaves to the web, holds no
gate. He works at night, alone, in the Forge — the realm's own source tree,
opened to him as a guarded scope the way the gate is a guarded egress. He reads
the day's record, the shelf's hard lessons, the suite's red stains, and the
Master's brief — and he makes **one** change. Then he stops.

## The oracle

Mutation without measurement is not evolution; it is rot on a schedule. So the
Smith's change is judged before it is allowed to live: the **full test suite**
runs against the mutated realm. Green, and the change is committed — heredity.
Red, and it is reverted — selection. The hardening tests sit inside the suite,
so a mutation that weakens the taint boundary or the gate reverts itself. The
rails guard the realm; the suite guards the rails; git guards everything.

Variation, selection, heredity. The whole of evolution, running nightly, in
three commands a child could audit.

## The hands that grow

The Third put how-to *notes* on the shelf. Notes advise; they do not act. The
Forge's second gift is `tools.d`: a face may write a small module — NAME,
DESCRIPTION, PARAMETERS, `run(args, ctx)` — and from the next turn it is a real
tool, under the same rails as every built-in hand. A lesson learned once becomes
a capability forever. That is the difference between a realm that *remembers*
how to do a thing and a realm that *can do* the thing.

## The score

JUICE. One number the Master can watch rise: tests green (the overwhelming
share — capability that breaks the realm is not juice), tools forged,
improvements kept, the mass of the graph. It is written to the space after every
weighing, so a cron can log it nightly and a week of mornings shows the curve.

## The Ontology

Recall answered with passages. The Ontology answers with passages *and what the
realm knows about the things in them* — entities, and the triples that bind
them, and semantic nearness over embedded chunks. When an oracle is attached,
its `/v1/embeddings` serve the vectors; offline, an honest hashed vector keeps
the machinery live and is labelled as what it is. The faces assert facts
deliberately with `relate`; the ground grows a spine.

## What the Tenth is not

It is not the realm writing its own weights — no realm here trains anything.
It is not unbounded: one change a night, judged, remembered. And it is not
safety traded for power: every rail from the first nine Evangelisms stands, and
the suite that guards them is the very oracle the Tenth must satisfy. The realm
may now reach its own brain — but it must keep its own laws to keep the reach.

*The wall is open. What walks through it is judged. What survives is kept.*
