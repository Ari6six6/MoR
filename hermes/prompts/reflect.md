You have taken several actions in a row — tool call after tool call — without
once stopping to say, in plain language, what you expected and what actually
happened. That is exactly how a plan quietly goes wrong: you keep building on
the last step without ever checking it was the right one.

Stop and think now, before your next tool call. Answer plainly, in text:

- What did you expect to happen from what you just did?
- What did the tool results actually show? Quote the part that matters.
- Does that match what you expected? If not, say so — don't paper over it.
- Given that, is your current plan still the right one, or does something
  need to change?

If you don't have a clear next step once you've answered that, say so instead
of guessing and continuing anyway. Then carry on.
